THE FRAMEWORK

The Six Thinking Hats

🤍

The Facts Hat

Clarity first
We start with clarity.

What is the client asking for?Who is the audience?What is the goal?
We gather all the necessary information: content pillars, structured calendars, and consistent posting strategies.
💙

The Process Hat

Defining the direction
This is where we define the goal.

We bring everything together and ensure the final direction is clear and aligned.Is it to sell? Inform? Or build the brand?Does this idea meet the objective?Does it align with the brand?
THE FRAMEWORK

The Six Thinking Hats

❤️

The Emotions Hat

Tone and connection
We define how the content should feel.

Human, relatable, and easy to connect with.Should this feel warm, reflective, exciting, or reassuring?
This shapes our tone and voice — the emotional architecture of every piece.
💛

The Value Hat

Meaning and purpose
This hat ensures the message has meaning.

Why should the audience care?What are they gaining from this?
Every piece of content must deliver value to earn the audience's attention and trust.
THE FRAMEWORK

The Six Thinking Hats

💚

The Creative Hat

Innovation and storytelling
We explore different directions.

Moving away from generic content to storytelling through airline roles.How can we say this in a way that stands out?
This is where ideas like storytelling and unique formats come in — the spark that makes content unforgettable.
🖤

The Critique Hat

Quality and alignment
We step back and critique the idea.

Is it clear?Is it relevant?Can it be misunderstood?
This is where we review and filter ideas, removing what doesn't align or connect with the brand's truth.
04

Thinking to Execution

How this thinking reflects in our work for Aero.

✍️
Storytelling Approach
Moved away from generic quotes and rethought the approach entirely.
  • Used airline roles as storytelling devices
  • Wrote in first-person for relatability
  • Made content more human and connectable
✈️
Destination Content
Changed how destinations were presented to spark genuine travel desire.
  • Focused on destinations, not just cities
  • Highlighted experiences over locations
  • Wrote to make audiences want to go there
