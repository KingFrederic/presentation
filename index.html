The Bottom Line

TL;DR

  • Cheapest verified taught-Master's options for international students starting 2026/27 are Wrexham University (£9,000 for most MA/MSc programmes, £11,000 for STEM/MBA), University of Cumbria (£16,000 list but ~£11,000 net after the automatic £5,000 PGT Global Excellence Scholarship), and Leeds Trinity University (£12,000 for arts/education/social-sciences MAs). Wrexham is the single cheapest official sticker price for a UK Master's that an international Nigerian can realistically be sponsored for in 2026/27.
  • For Alexander's profile (Botany BSc + business-development + entrepreneurship), the best-odds, fastest-decision, cheapest-net pick is MSc International Business Management OR MBA at Wrexham University (£9,000–£11,000), with MSc Conservation Management / MSc Forest Science at the University of Cumbria (~£11,000 net) as the science-track twin.   • The Nigerian-specific reality: every cheap UK university now requires a pre-CAS credibility interview, plus a £1,000–£5,000 (or 50%-of-fees) tuition deposit before issuing the CAS. UKVI requires £1,171/month × 9 = £10,539 in maintenance funds, held for 28 consecutive days. Plan ~£21,700–£23,700 total upfront cash (tuition + maintenance + £558 visa + £776 IHS + flights) on top of admission costs.
Section 01

Key Findings

  1. The cheapest 2026/27 international PGT tuition fees in the UK cluster in Welsh, North-West English and post-92 modern universities — particularly Wrexham, Cumbria (after scholarship), Leeds Trinity, Bedfordshire, Sunderland and Buckinghamshire New.
  2. Wrexham University is the lowest sticker price: £9,000/year for most arts, social-sciences, business and humanities Master's, and £11,000 for STEM/MBA. It is the only mainstream UK university charging international students below £10,000 for a taught Master's in 2026/27.
  3. Several "commonly cited cheap" universities have risen above £17,000: University of Bolton/Greater Manchester (£17,500), Teesside (£17,000), Anglia Ruskin (£17,500–£21,200), Birmingham City (£17,710) and London Met (£15,700–£17,500). They no longer sit in the lowest-tuition tier.
  4. No UK university publishes an official acceptance rate. Every percentage circulated by Collegedunia, Shiksha, LeapScholar and similar agents is a third-party estimate. UK modern/post-92 universities are nevertheless widely understood to issue offers to most applicants meeting the academic floor of 2:2 + IELTS 6.0–6.5.
  5. Only one cheap university gives an officially published admission-decision SLA: Sunderland commits to a decision within 2 working days for standard applications and 5 working days otherwise. London Met states "two to three weeks" as typical. All others rely on agent-reported turnaround.
  6. A pre-CAS credibility interview is mandatory at every cheap university surveyed for Nigerian applicants (Wrexham, Cumbria, Chester, BNU, BCU, London Met, Leeds Trinity, Sunderland, Bedfordshire). Nigeria is treated as a high-risk / enhanced-scrutiny country by every UK university.
  7. The single biggest 2026 planning fact: the UKVI maintenance threshold rose to £1,171/month outside London for visa applications submitted from 11 November 2025 onwards (UKCISA Statement of Changes notice) — i.e. all 2026/27 intake applications. Funds you must show = tuition balance + £10,539, seasoned for 28 consecutive days.
  8. The Graduate Route (post-study work visa) will shrink from 24 months to 18 months for non-PhD graduates from 1 January 2027 (House of Commons Library Research Briefing CBP-10267: "Graduate visas will only last 18 months if the person applies from 1 January 2027 (but 36 months if they have a PhD)"). Applications submitted on or before 31 December 2026 keep the 24-month right. This alone makes the September 2026 intake materially more valuable than January 2027 for someone planning to work in the UK post-degree.
  9. For a Botany graduate with business experience, classroom-based conversion Master's in International Business Management, Project Management, Sustainability/Environmental Management, or Conservation Management are the optimum fit — they build on the BSc, leverage the BD background, AND fall into the cheapest fee tier at every modern university.

Section 03

Application Process for the 2026/27 Intake (International)

Application route — postgraduate taught (PGT)

  • Direct to the university, in nearly all cases. UCAS Postgraduate exists but is not used by most post-92 universities; each runs its own portal (apply.wrexham.ac.uk, beds.ac.uk/howtoapply, aru.ac.uk's direct PG application, etc.).
  • Nigeria-specific channels: Leeds Trinity requires Nigerian applicants to go through its West Africa regional team (direct applications are rejected). Wrexham, BNU, BCU and London Met explicitly route Nigerian applicants through pre-CAS credibility interviews. Greater Manchester (Bolton) actively channels Nigerian applications via approved in-country agents.

Standard PGT documents required (international)

  • Bachelor's degree certificate + full academic transcript
  • CV / Résumé
  • Personal Statement (~500 words: why this course, why this university)
  • 1–2 academic or professional references
  • Copy of passport (biodata page)
  • English-language evidence (see below)
  • Some courses additionally require a portfolio or an interview (creative arts, social work, education)

English-language requirements for Nigerian applicants

  • Most cheap UK universities accept WAEC SSCE / NECO English at Credit (C6) or better as English-language proof for Nigerian applicants — explicitly stated on the University of Greater Manchester (Bolton) international application page ("Applicants from Nigeria/Africa must upload their WAEC or NECO certificate before the application is submitted"). The same Medium-of-Instruction (MOI) waiver is offered by Wrexham, Bedfordshire, BNU, Chester, and Leeds Trinity for applicants from English-medium institutions.
  • Where IELTS is required, the standard threshold is IELTS 6.0–6.5 overall with 5.5 in each band. Common alternatives: TOEFL iBT 78–90, PTE Academic 56–62, Duolingo English Test 105–120. University of Bedfordshire confirmed (25 June 2025) it accepts Duolingo 110 (IELTS 6.0 equivalent) and 120 (IELTS 6.5 equivalent).

Entry-grade requirements relevant to a Nigerian BSc holder

  • The standard PGT entry floor is a UK 2:2 Honours equivalent — at Obafemi Awolowo University this maps to ~3.00/5.00 CGPA (Second Class Lower) or 50–59% in NUC-graded modules.
  • Modern/post-92 universities will frequently accept a Third Class or Pass degree with relevant work experience, an interview, or via a Pre-Master's foundation route (offered by Wrexham, BNU, Cumbria, BCU, Bedfordshire, Sunderland and Teesside).
  • An MBA may require 1–2 years' management experience (Bedfordshire's MBA does); Wrexham, BNU and BCU usually waive this for the International MBA variant.

Intake cycles

  • September 2026 — main intake (broadest course list).
  • January 2027 — secondary intake (widely offered: Wrexham, Bedfordshire, Cumbria, Sunderland, Teesside, ARU, Greater Manchester, BNU, BCU, London Met — fewer courses).
  • May/June 2027 — limited (Teesside, BNU April, Sunderland November/January).

Typical timeline for September 2026

  • Applications open: August–October 2025 (mostly already open at this report's date, 16 May 2026).
  • Recommended apply-by: April–May 2026 for September 2026 (Nigerian students need ~10–14 weeks for offer → deposit → CAS → 28-day fund-seasoning → biometrics → visa decision).
  • Deposit/CAS deadline (typical): 21 August 2026 (Leeds Trinity, Cumbria, Bedfordshire).
  • Visa application: from CAS issue; no later than 6 weeks before course start.
  • Course start: mid-September 2026.
  • For January 2027 intake: apply by late October 2026; deposit/CAS by December 2026; visa decisions early January 2027.

CAS, deposit, visa & maintenance funds (2026/27)

  • CAS is issued after you accept your unconditional offer, pay the tuition deposit, and pass any pre-CAS interview.
  • Standard tuition deposit by university (verified on official pages):
    • Lowest initial outlay: Wrexham £1,000 (refundable interview deposit) and BNU £1,000 (refundable interview deposit).
    • Standard first-stage CAS deposit: £3,500 (Sunderland) → £4,000 (Teesside, Cumbria, London Met) → £5,000 (Chester).
    • 50% of first-year tuition fees required at Bedfordshire, Leeds Trinity, BCU (after the £1,000 interview deposit), BNU (after interview), Teesside (typical), and Chester for high-risk countries.
  • UK Student visa application fee 2026: £558 — increased from £524 with effect from 8 April 2026 (gov.uk/student-visa; confirmed by DavidsonMorris immigration firm: "Student visa fees increased from £524 to £558").
  • Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS): £776 per year of study (paid in full upfront — so £776 for a one-year Master's).
  • Maintenance fund requirement (critical 2026 update): for visa applications submitted on or after 11 November 2025 — i.e. every 2026/27 intake applicant — UKCISA's official Student Update confirms verbatim: "£1,171 per month outside London (up to a maximum of 9 months)" = £10,539, held in your (or your parent's) personal bank account for 28 consecutive days ending no more than 31 days before the date you submit the visa application. For London courses: £1,483/month = £13,347. (Several university web pages — notably BCU's "International Application FAQs" and parts of Teesside's international pages — still display the older £1,023/£1,136 figures: rely only on gov.uk/student-visa.)
  • TB-test certificate is mandatory for Nigerian applicants — book at an IOM-approved clinic in Lagos, Abuja or Port Harcourt (valid for 6 months).
  • Visa processing time from Nigeria: ~3 weeks standard from biometrics; Priority service ~5 working days for an additional fee. Home Office full-year 2025 data show Nigeria's student visa grant rate at 92% (StudyTravel Network analysis), with 36,839 Nigerian study-visa grants for the year ending September 2025 (Legit.ng citing Home Office Immigration System Statistics).

Caveat: visa applications submitted from 1 January 2027 carry only an 18-month Graduate Route, so the post-study work runway shrinks materially.
  • If the pre-CAS credibility interview is a concern (some Nigerian applicants are rejected at this stage): Sunderland (officially 2-working-day decision, £3,500 deposit) and Bedfordshire (£1,000 PGT Vice-Chancellor's auto-scholarship; high acceptance) are the safer next picks.
Section 09

Caveats

  1. No UK university publishes an official "acceptance rate". Every percentage in this report from Collegedunia, Shiksha, LeapScholar, AECC and similar aggregators is a third-party estimate. Treat them as directional, not authoritative. The strongest signal is qualitative: modern post-92 universities (Wrexham, Cumbria, Bedfordshire, Sunderland, Bolton/Greater Manchester, BNU, Chester, BCU, ARU, London Met) are widely understood to issue offers to most applicants who meet the 2:2-Honours-plus-IELTS-6.0 floor.
  2. Decision-speed claims should be treated cautiously. Only Sunderland (2 working days standard) and London Met (2–3 weeks typical) publish official SLAs on their own .ac.uk sites. The other "1–2 weeks" / "10 working days" figures come from agent partners or aggregator sites — Nigerian applications are routinely slower than the published figure because of the mandatory pre-CAS credibility interview and additional compliance scrutiny.
  3. The UKVI maintenance figure changed on 11 November 2025 from £1,023/month to £1,171/month outside London (£1,529/month in London) — confirmed in UKCISA's official Student Update of October 2025 ("£1171 per month outside London (up to a maximum of 9 months)") and corroborated by City St George's University of London's immigration news page citing the same Statement of Changes. Several university web pages still display the old figure — rely only on gov.uk/student-visa as authoritative. As of May 2026, the figure for all 2026/27 intake visa applications is £1,171/month (£10,539 for nine months).
  4. The UK Student visa application fee rose to £558 from 8 April 2026 (DavidsonMorris immigration firm citing gov.uk: "Student visa fees increased from £524 to £558"). Older blog references still quote £524 — these are out of date.
  5. 2026/27 fees are confirmed for Wrexham, Cumbria, Bedfordshire, Sunderland, Leeds Trinity, Greater Manchester (Bolton), Anglia Ruskin, BCU and Chester. For Teesside and UWS, the 2026/27 number either equals 2025/26 or is still under review at the date of this report — verify directly with the university before relying on it for visa planning.
  6. Leeds Trinity University requires Nigerian applicants to apply via its West Africa team, not directly. Direct applications are explicitly stated as "not considered". This adds 1–2 weeks to the decision turnaround and means you cannot use UCAS or the direct portal for this institution.
  7. Pre-CAS credibility interviews are mandatory at Wrexham, BNU, BCU, London Met, Chester (for Nigeria), Cumbria (50%-deposit countries) and Bedfordshire (high-risk countries). Prepare carefully — failed interviews are a common cause of CAS refusal for Nigerian applicants.
  8. The UK Graduate Route (post-study work visa) will shrink from 24 months to 18 months for non-PhD graduates from 1 January 2027 — House of Commons Library Research Briefing CBP-10267 states verbatim: "Graduate visas will only last 18 months if the person applies from 1 January 2027 (but 36 months if they have a PhD)" (confirmed by UKCISA). Visa applications submitted on or before 31 December 2026 retain the 24-month right. This is a strong reason to target September 2026 entry rather than January 2027.
  9. Tuition fees may rise from 2028/29. University of Chester's own international fees page (updated March 2026) states verbatim: "Tuition fees will increase by £925 per year from the 2028/29 academic year, reflecting the Government's proposed international student fees levy." The underlying levy — charged to institutions, not students — was announced in the UK Autumn Budget of 26 November 2025 (Times Higher Education). The 2026/27 fees in this report are likely the cheapest year you will see in the UK for a Master's for a Nigerian self-funded applicant for the foreseeable future.

One last thing, Alexander

The window that matters most is the September 2026 intake. Applying and getting your visa decision on or before 31 December 2026 preserves the full 24-month post-study work route — from 1 January 2027 it drops to 18 months. Everything else in this dossier is negotiable; that deadline isn't.

Start with two free applications today: Wrexham University and University of Cumbria. Then build the proof-of-funds while the offers come in.
